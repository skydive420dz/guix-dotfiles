* Why the extra source checkpoint is required

The previous roadmap said that D4c.1 should both use a reviewed collector and
publish a manifest that binds the program-source checkpoint.  No production
collector or consumer existed.  Generating the manifest from uncommitted
source would violate clean-published provenance; committing source and
manifest together would make the source-checkpoint binding circular.

The corrected D4c.1a -> D4c.1b ordering removes that cycle.  The manifest will
bind an already published source checkpoint and exact regular-file
SHA256/size identities.  Its bytes still omit its own SHA256, the future
program store path/hash, and the final direct program-root target.

* Bounded protocol currently enforced

The current schema is =p5.2b-system-prune-live-manifest/v1=.  It requires
canonical ordered TSV with a final newline and fixes:

- =mode LIVE-REVIEW-ONLY=, =status REVIEW-ONLY=, and
  =authorization NOT-GRANTED=;
- source, D4b evidence, D4b validator, pinned Guix, and pinned Guile
  identities;
- exact program-source path/SHA256/size records;
- every System/Home/Pull generation raw/canonical tuple and every profile or
  runtime pointer;
- the D-34 selected/retained System partition, exact pin tuples, and exact
  count transition;
- the pin-file identity, installed GRUB, retained-only GRUB, old/new
  =bootcfg=, and successful forward =COMPLETE= expected postflight;
- a non-following EFI-tree inventory, selected boot-variable records, and
  explicit one-shot-variable absence;
- absent transaction/root/lock/temporary prestates and manifest-hash formulas;
- one recovery-root declaration per selected System link plus old/new
  =bootcfg=;
- the exact zero-argument =live-verify=, =live-apply=, and =live-recover=
  interface;
- absent execution and recovery grant tokens;
- the currently declared successful-forward
  journal/root/lock/transaction policy; and
- explicit prohibitions plus =program-build/lowering/realization NOT-RUN= and
  =live-action NOT-GRANTED=.

The validator rejects unknown, missing, duplicated, reordered, malformed, or
unsafe records; partition/count/root drift; manifest self-reference; a future
program target; formula expansion; incomplete EFI or declared forward
postflight policy; action or grant widening; and any effect claim.

This bounded checkpoint does not yet freeze the final production
=source-input= label/path inventory or exact retained-GRUB artifact path:
several required adapter, collector, and driver files do not exist yet.  The
current validator closes record shape/uniqueness/order only.  D4c.1a must
freeze the complete exact inventory after those additive sources exist and
before D4c.1b captures a manifest.

Likewise, the declared postflight rows describe only a successful forward
=COMPLETE= transaction.  The complete =ROLLED-BACK= postflight branch and the
terminal disposition of =/var/guix/gcroots/p52b-system-prune= remain open
production-adapter decisions.  They must be frozen and tested before D4c.1a
closes; D4c.1b remains blocked in the meantime.

* Production-source gap

The adapter review stopped safely because the existing D4a interfaces are
deliberately closed over the synthetic fixture:

- =(sk system-pruning-boundary)= rejects any mode other than =FIXTURE-ONLY=
  and emits a fixture-only journal header;
- =(sk system-pruning-phase-engine)= calls that validator unconditionally;
- =(sk system-pruning-reconciliation)= requires =FIXTURE-ONLY=,
  =NOT-GRANTED=, a non-root sentinel fixture, and fixture-root descendants,
  and exports only =sk:reconcile-synthetic!=; and
- the design describes D5 grant semantics but has no exact token byte
  encoding/parser.

The correct successor is additive live-specific source.  It must preserve the
frozen fixture files, define the live boundary/journal/reconciliation and
grant grammar, implement the injected production adapter, then add the
collector and fused renderer/driver.  It must also freeze the complete
source-input inventory, =ROLLED-BACK= postflight branch, and recovery-root-base
terminal disposition.  D4c.1a remains BACKLOG until those gates are complete
and published.
